Option 3: Development/Redevelopment Projects

Investment Strategy Name: NM22

Min Investment: Â£5,000,000

New developments are mostly sold out before completion, however sale cannot progress unless the project is nearly ready with a set completion date. There is no law that governs when the inventory can be sold, but delaying handover typically cost a lot in lawsuits so economically it makes sense to sale once we have completion date set.

The Positives

  • No hassle of tenant handling, meaning no tenancy laws to comply with.
  • Typically, bigger profit margins.
  • Upwards of 30% returns in 2 years meaning 15% anual returns.
  • Projections can be made for 2+ years however in worst case if there is a downtrend at around completion date, the sale of inventory can take longer.
  • Option to keep the freehold rights of the compound while selling individual leaseholds
  • In downward market, there are various ways to handle the situation:
    • Selling partial equities in the house for easy affordability.
    • Joining government schemes that help buyers.
    • Letting out some units for some time while the market is not active.
